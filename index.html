A TRUE FIGHTER FOR HUMAN RIGHTS

Nelson Mandela,was the first democratically elected President of post Apartheid South Africa.He became President after serving 27 years in prison of the required sentenced 30 years for opposing the white minority government.He was released on 11th February 1990.

Four years later on 27th June,he became President and resigned in 1999 continued his advocacy in human rights.

He died on 05th December 2013 at the age of 95 from a lung infection but he left a legacy which is still a challenge for us to learn from and uphold.

WHAT A CHARACTER!

Nelson Mandela,was an impeccable man and fascinatingly modest in his approach to the Presidency in contrast to the ill treatment he was subjected to,including hard labour that he endured while in prison.

The outgoing white minority government when he became President was weary of the governemt policies he was going to put in place thinking,they were going to be vindictive with vengeance,but he turned a new leaf advocating for a truthful reconciliation for a unified South Africa.
